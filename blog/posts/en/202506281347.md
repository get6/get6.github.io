"Fundraising" -- it's one of those words that doesn't really hit home until you're in a pinch. I'm currently going through the 2025 Pre-Startup Package program, but the reality is that it's daunting when you think about all the expenses ahead. It wasn't easy to figure out how to raise money or even find experts to get advice from.

## Fundraising: The First Step to Startup Success

Capital is like the lifeblood of a business. I believe that if you build a systematic funding plan from the early startup stages, anyone can lead their business to success. Remember -- financial management is both the beginning and the end of running a company!

### Warning Signs to Watch For

Cash shortages show up when you least expect them.

To avoid these risks:

- **Start planning your finances at least 6 months ahead**
- Prepare for potential delays in prototype evaluation periods (Chasm)
- Visualize monthly performance with Waterfall charts to track cash flow
- Minimize fixed costs to prevent technical bankruptcy

### When You'll Need Funding

Funding needs change at each growth stage, not just at launch:

1. **Technology development / prototype creation**
2. Securing production funds for mass manufacturing
3. Working capital due to increasing accounts receivable
4. Backup for cash burn during business delays
5. Additional funds for expanding equipment

### Effective Fundraising Strategy

Prepare systematically with this **5-step approach**:

1. **Cash flow plan**: Separate short-term (operations) from mid/long-term (investment)
2. **Identify required funding amounts and timing**
3. **Decide on funding methods**: Compare pros and cons, factor in market conditions
4. **Write business plan and applications**
5. **Target specific agencies**

**Purposes of Government Policy Funds**:

- Industrial development
- Building a sound industrial structure and balanced regional growth
- Job creation and expansion, promoting technology development
- Encouraging domestic R&D
- Strengthening international competitiveness, import substitution, and export promotion

### Funding Methods Perfect for Founders

**Top 3 sources recommended for early-stage startups**:

1. **Government Policy Funds** (Korea SMEs and Startups Agency, Seoul Business Agency, etc.): Low interest rates, favorable repayment terms
2. **Angel Investment**: Suitable during the technology validation stage
3. **Venture Capital (VC)**: Large-scale investment after growth is confirmed

### You Can Do This

Fundraising isn't an insurmountable wall.
If you prepare systematically starting 6 months ahead and leverage startup-friendly programs like government policy funds, you can definitely make it happen.
Getting the first button right is the start of a successful business.
Start by writing a cash flow plan today!

> **Recommended Action Plan**:
>
> 1. Write a cash flow statement at the end of every month
> 2. Visit local startup support centers (Seoul Business Agency, etc.)
> 3. Apply within the limits of the Korea Technology Finance Corporation
