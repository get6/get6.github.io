We've also developed a proprietary flash memory controller architecture to build ultra-low-power, ultra-high-performance SSDs. The core value proposition is delivering consistent QoS (Quality of Service) that goes beyond the limitations of legacy architectures.

FADU addresses supply chain bottlenecks with an innovative model that includes turnkey solutions (complete products), custom firmware, and BOM (Bill of Materials). This helps OEMs and cloud providers produce quickly and respond to NAND price fluctuations.

## Flash Controller -- Technology That Makes Good Flash Even Faster

SSD performance varies greatly not just from the NAND flash itself but from **controller technology**.
The controller efficiently manages NAND flash and optimizes **speed, reliability, and lifespan**.
Think of it as a **librarian who precisely organizes data and manages errors**.

Controllers are essential not just for SSDs but also for
various flash solutions including **embedded MultiMedia Cards (eMMC)**.

## Flash Memory Basics

Flash memory is a **non-volatile semiconductor storage device** that retains data even when power is off.
The name 'Flash' comes from how it **operates quickly**, like a camera flash.

**Advantages**

- Fast speed, small and lightweight, low power consumption

**Disadvantages**

- Can only rewrite in block units
- Limited number of write cycles
- Improper handling can cause data corruption

### Interleaving

A technique that **boosts speed** by distributing data across multiple memory chips in parallel.
It's a form of 'interleaved processing' that splits traffic to improve error recovery and transfer efficiency.

### Gate Structures

Flash is divided into **NAND** and **NOR** types.

- **NAND:** For processing large amounts of data quickly (e.g., SSDs, memory cards)
- **NOR:** For fast read speeds and random access needs (e.g., firmware storage)

NAND is like "stacking piles of documents high" -- large capacity and efficient.
NOR is like "a neatly indexed filing cabinet" -- great accessibility.

## FTL -- Flash Translation Layer

![](/assets/202512292132-1767018669903.webp)

FTL is the **core technology that maps data between flash memory and the file system**.
This technology is what makes it possible to **use flash like a regular hard drive**.
Block erasing, wear-leveling, garbage collection, and other SSD internal operations all happen at this layer.

## Product Lineup

### BRAVO -- The Starting Point of Efficient Performance

- Interface: PCIe 3.0 x4
- Spec: NVMe 1.4 | OCP NVMe Cloud SSD 1.0

The BRAVO platform delivers **up to 2x KIOPS with 30% less power**.
As the **industry's first RISC-V-based controller**, it achieves stable performance without throttling.
Available in E1.S and M.2 SSD form factors, offered as turnkey and Private Label solutions.

---

### DELTA -- 2nd Generation High-Performance Platform

- Interface: PCIe 4.0 x4
- Spec: NVMe 1.4b | OCP NVMe Cloud Spec 1.0a

DELTA achieves **2x performance improvement** and even lower power consumption compared to the BRAVO platform.
Based on FADU's **2nd generation FC4121 controller**, it delivers industry-leading **KIOPS/Watt**.
Available in E1.S and U.2 SSD form factors.

---

### ECHO -- Next-Generation PCIe 5.0 Platform

- Interface: PCIe 5.0 x4 (x2 Dual Port)
- Spec: NVMe 2.0 | OCP Datacenter NVMe SSD 2.0

The **ECHO platform is now available.**
ECHO is designed for next-generation hyperscale, cloud, edge, and enterprise data centers.
Equipped with the latest **FC5161 controller**, it delivers extremely low latency and high QoS,
while virtually eliminating thermal throttling issues.

It also supports **FDP (Flexible Data Placement)** and **64 Physical Functions**,
positioning itself as the **industry's first OCP-certified SSD** to integrate these capabilities.
