## What Is Entrepreneurship? The Mindset of a True Entrepreneur

A lot of people get confused between the terms **businessman**, **startup founder**, and **entrepreneur**.
In this post, I'll clarify these concepts, explore what entrepreneurship really means, and look at what skills and attitudes you need to become a successful founder!

**The Difference Between a Businessman, a Startup Founder, and an Entrepreneur**:

- **Businessman**: Someone who runs a business in an existing market with the goal of **generating profit**
- **Entrepreneur (Startup Founder)**: Someone who **discovers new opportunities and creates a new business**. They pioneer new business models or markets that didn't exist before, creating new value
- **Entrepreneur (with Entrepreneurial Spirit)**: Going beyond just running a business, this is someone who **discovers new value and opportunities, and challenges themselves to realize them through creative and innovative methods**. An entrepreneur aims to make a positive impact on the world through continuous innovation and change, rather than chasing short-term profits

**Capitalism and the Role of Entrepreneurs**:
Capitalism evolves through **producing new goods, improving existing product quality, introducing new production methods, and pioneering new markets**. At the center of all this is the **entrepreneur**.

**Entrepreneurs are the drivers of innovation who create value and industries that never existed before, through creative destruction.**

### Core Elements of the Early Startup Process

For a successful startup, these four elements need to be organically connected:

1. **Discovering startup opportunities**
2. **Securing resources**
3. **Building a team**
4. **The founder's role**

In the early startup process, full of **uncertainty and risk**, the founder's leadership and problem-solving abilities are especially critical.

## The Founder's Role

- **Writing a business plan**
  Turning ideas into concrete, actionable plans
- **Active communication**
  Communicating clearly and persuasively with investors, team members, customers, and other stakeholders
- **Demonstrating leadership**
  Unifying the team, sharing the vision, and providing motivation
- **Creative problem solving**
  Solving unexpected problems by breaking out of conventional thinking

## What Is Entrepreneurship?

Entrepreneurship goes beyond just a mindset or attitude.
It means **creative and proactive action** that creates value the world has never seen, through the process of **thinking -> executing -> solving problems -> creating value**.

### Core Elements of Entrepreneurship

1. **Achievement Drive**
   The desire to reach high goals. The driving force to keep challenging yourself without fearing failure
2. **Innovation**
   Introducing ideas and methods that didn't exist before to lead change
3. **Risk Tolerance**
   Boldly seizing opportunities even in uncertain situations
4. **Proactiveness**
   Recognizing change on your own, creating opportunities, and preparing for the future

### Characteristics of People Full of Entrepreneurship

- **They find opportunities in problems that others overlook**
- **They solve problems innovatively and creatively**
- **They take on risk and execute boldly**
- **They work autonomously and independently**
- **They have a strong drive to achieve their goals**
- **They push forward passionately despite lack of funding and resources**
- **They don't give up in the face of crises and obstacles -- they push through with persistence**
- **They challenge themselves, understanding that opportunity lives within crisis**

These are people who have realized that **obstacles and opportunities are separated by a razor-thin line**.

True entrepreneurship isn't about grand theories or fancy quotes.
It's the process of **finding opportunities in the small problems you face every day, taking action yourself, hitting walls, failing, and trying again**.

**"Stay hungry, stay foolish"**
As Steve Jobs said, if you're a founder, you should always crave new things and have the foolish courage to never hesitate before a challenge.

If you have some idea or concern on your mind right now, don't hesitate -- just go for it.
**That's exactly where entrepreneurship begins.**
